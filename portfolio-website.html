About Me

I am a PhD student in Geography and the Environment at the University of Texas at Austin, with a strong background in Urban & Regional Planning. My research focuses on environmental sustainability, climate resilience, and the application of geospatial technologies to address complex environmental challenges.

With expertise in Geographic Information Systems (GIS), remote sensing, and machine learning algorithms, I work on developing innovative approaches to model and analyze land use changes, urban heat islands, and ecological risk assessment.

Throughout my career, I have collaborated with international organizations like ICLEI - Local Governments for Sustainability, contributing to climate action plans and greenhouse gas emission inventories for urban areas.

I am passionate about creating sustainable urban environments and using data-driven approaches to inform environmental policy and decision-making.
